The Challenge
98%

Fail to cross the
Blood-Brain Barrier

Over 98% of small molecules and ~100% of large biologics fail to reach the CNS in therapeutic concentrations. Researchers spend months in wet-lab trial-and-error when the answer could be computed in minutes.

  • The BBB actively rejects 95%+ of candidate nanocarriers via efflux pumps and enzymatic degradation
  • Protein corona formation silently deactivates 70% of "successful" in-vitro formulations in vivo
  • Scale-up from lab to manufacturing introduces shear stress that collapses most nanoparticle architectures
  • Drug-DDS compatibility is rarely assessed computationally — leading to encapsulation failures discovered only after synthesis
  • Glymphatic clearance removes brain-resident carriers 4–10× faster than researchers assume, negating CNS residence time

The Framework

Powered by 62 Validation Principles

Every candidate DDS is evaluated against 62 computational principles. Three pipeline classes, eight CNS pharma dimensions, one unified composite score.

CNS Delivery

BBB transcytosis, tight junction dynamics, efflux pump interactions, receptor-mediated crossing

Release Kinetics

Dissolution profiles, sustained vs burst release, API-DDS compatibility scoring

Stability

Protein corona, polymorph transformation, shelf-life under Arrhenius kinetics

Safety

hERG, complement activation, microglial neuroinflammation, ADMET off-target toxicity

Glymphatic

CSF clearance trap, Stokes-Einstein diffusion, sleep-cycle-dependent drainage

Manufacturing

Scale-up shear stress, lyophilisation cycles, sterilisability, QbD compliance

Drug-DDS Fit

Ionisation state, encapsulation thermodynamics, ligand-receptor docking (AutoDock Vina)

Translational

Pre-IND framework, FTO & patentability scoring, FDA 21 CFR Part 11 audit trail
